From "reached" to "stopped at"

The ladder above counts how high people climbed, so the groups overlap — a completer is also counted in Tier 2 and Tier 1. To turn that into lots, each person has to be counted once, grouped by where they stopped. One rule does it:

Stopped at a tier = reached that tier − the ones who climbed past it
climbed past — counted in a higher tier stopped here — this is who trades at this tier
Tier 1
890 − 556 = 334
Tier 2
556 − 371 = 185
Tier 3 · top
371 − 0 = 371
Why completers doesn't change: the top is the end of the road — nothing climbs higher than Tier 3, so there's no one to subtract (− 0). Everyone who reached the top also stopped at the top. That's why completers stays the same in both stages, while the two tiers below shrink once you remove the climbers who went past them.
